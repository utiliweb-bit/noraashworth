Literary Fiction · A Novel

The Weight of Her Silence

A locked back room. A cedar box. A name no one ever said out loud. When Nora drives four hours south from Atlanta to help her seventy-eight-year-old aunt sort through the past, she expects dust and old furniture. She does not expect a name that rewrites everything her mother ever believed about who she is. A quiet, emotionally precise novel about the secrets families keep, the cost of protection that becomes erasure, and what it means to finally say the true thing.

About the Author

Nora Ashworth

Nora Ashworth writes about the quiet architecture of families — the protective lies, the letters never sent, the names people carry that were never quite their own. Her novels move between continents and generations, but return again and again to the same question: what do we owe the people who kept a silence for our sake?
